A Brief History of Thanksgiving

Thanksgiving is a national holiday in the United States, celebrated on the fourth Thursday of November every year. The tradition of Thanksgiving dates back to 1621, when the Pilgrims, early European colonizers of the United States, held a harvest feast to celebrate their first successful crop.

The Wampanoag Native American tribe had helped the Pilgrims adapt to their new environment and taught them how to farm. The feast lasted for three days and was attended by around 50 Pilgrims and 90 Wampanoag guests.

In 1789, George Washington, the first president of the United States, declared a national day of thanksgiving to commemorate the adoption of the United States Constitution. However, it wasn't until 1863, during the Civil War, that Thanksgiving became a national holiday, when President Abraham Lincoln declared it a day of thanksgiving and praise.

Thanksgiving Traditions

  • Family Gatherings

    Thanksgiving is a time for families to come together and share a meal. Many people travel long distances to be with their loved ones on this special day.

  • Traditional Foods

    The traditional Thanksgiving meal typically includes roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, stuffing, cranberry sauce, and pumpkin pie.

  • Parades and Football Games

    Many cities and towns host parades and football games on Thanksgiving Day. The most famous parade is the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade in New York City.

  • Volunteering

    Many people volunteer at soup kitchens, food banks, and other organizations that help those in need on Thanksgiving Day.

Fun Facts About Thanksgiving

  • The First Thanksgiving Was Not Just About Food

    The first Thanksgiving was also a time for the Pilgrims and the Wampanoag to come together and celebrate their friendship and alliance.

  • Thanksgiving Was Not Always a National Holiday

    Thanksgiving did not become a national holiday until 1863, when President Abraham Lincoln declared it a day of thanksgiving and praise.

  • The Tradition of Watching Football on Thanksgiving Started in 1934

    The Detroit Lions have been hosting a Thanksgiving Day football game since 1934, and it has become a beloved tradition for many Americans.

  • The Average American Eats 4,500 Calories on Thanksgiving

    That's a lot of turkey, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie!

Thanksgiving Activities

  • Volunteer at a Soup Kitchen

    Many soup kitchens and food banks need volunteers to help serve meals and sort donations on Thanksgiving Day.

  • Watch a Football Game

    Many people enjoy watching football games on Thanksgiving Day. You can watch a game with your family and friends, or attend a live game if possible.

  • Take a Walk or Go for a Run

    After eating a big meal, it's a good idea to get some exercise. Take a walk or go for a run to help digest your food and get some fresh air.

  • Play Games or Do Puzzles

    Many people enjoy playing games or doing puzzles on Thanksgiving Day. You can play board games, card games, or video games, or work on a puzzle with your family and friends.
